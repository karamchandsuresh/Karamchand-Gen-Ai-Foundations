The Bag-of-Words model may show similarity between Sentence 1 and Sentence 2 even though the word "light" has different meanings. CountVectorizer only counts occurrences of words and does not understand context. Because both sentences contain the word "light", the vectors overlap mathematically. This creates a false similarity score between sentences with different meanings.
Bag-of-Words loses contextual information and word order when converting text into vectors. Every occurrence of a word receives the same representation regardless of meaning. This limitation makes search engines and chatbots less accurate because they cannot distinguish different meanings of the same word. Semantic context is completely lost.
Modern LLMs such as GPT and LLaMA use contextual embeddings and self-attention mechanisms. Self-attention analyzes surrounding words to determine the correct meaning of a word in a sentence. Therefore, the word "light" in a food-related sentence receives a different vector than "light" in an illumination-related sentence. This enables accurate language understanding and generation.
